World Music Genres

Explore a selection of diverse music genres from around the world. Click on a genre to discover more about its unique origins. This does not represent all music genres globally but have shaped the musical scenes.

Genre Origin Description
Afrobeat Nigeria A combination of traditional Nigerian music, jazz, highlife, funk, and chanted vocals, fused with percussion and vocal styles.
Bhangra India A type of popular music combining Punjabi folk traditions with Western pop music.
Bossa Nova Brazil A style of Brazilian music derived from samba but placing more emphasis on melody and less on percussion.
Calypso Trinidad and Tobago A style of Afro-Caribbean music that originated in Trinidad and Tobago during the early to mid-19th century and spread to the rest of the Caribbean Antilles and Venezuela.
Chanson France A lyric-driven French song genre, chanson is noted for its poetic, often melancholic melodies and complex lyrics focusing on romance, life's trials, and political tales.
Country USA A genre of popular music that originated in the southern United States in the early 1920s.
Cumbia Colombia A folkloric genre and dance from Colombia and Panama, primarily derived from the African and indigenous mixed music traditions.
Electronic Dance Music (EDM) Various A broad range of percussive electronic music genres made largely for nightclubs, raves, and festivals.
Fado Portugal A music genre that can be traced back to the 1820s in Lisbon, which is characterized by mournful tunes and lyrics.
Flamenco Spain Known for its emotional intensity, flamenco is a traditional Spanish art form that includes singing, guitar playing, dance, and handclaps.
Folk Music Various Traditional music that originates from the shared culture and traditions of a community or country, often passed down orally through generations.
Gagaku Japan Traditional music of the Japanese imperial court, it is one of the oldest types of music in Japan.
Highlife Ghana A musical genre that originated in Ghana in the early 20th century, it combines traditional Akan rhythms with Western instruments.
J-Pop Japan A loosely defined musical genre that entered the musical mainstream of Japan in the 1990s, it is modern pop and rock music with a strong Japanese cultural influence.
Jazz USA Originated in the late 19th and early 20th centuries in the Southern United States, jazz is known for its swing and blue notes, complex chords, and call and response vocals.
K-pop South Korea A genre of popular music originating in South Korea that is characterized by a wide variety of audiovisual elements.
Klezmer Eastern Europe A musical tradition of the Ashkenazi Jews of Eastern Europe, characterized typically by its expressive melodies reminiscent of the human voice.
Mariachi Mexico A style of music and musical group performance that dates back to at least the 18th century, evolving over time in the countryside of various regions of western Mexico.
Polka Central Europe A fast-paced dance and genre of dance music familiar throughout Europe and the Americas.
Pop USA, UK Originating in the mid-1950s as a softer alternative to rock 'n' roll, Pop music is characterized by its catchy melodies and choruses, mainstream appeal, and widespread commercial success. It often incorporates elements from other styles such as urban, dance, rock, Latin, and country.
Qawwali Pakistan A form of Sufi Islamic devotional music originating from the Indian subcontinent, notable for its intense vocal style.
Reggae Jamaica A music style that originated in Jamaica in the late 1960s, characterized by a strong rhythmic emphasis on the off-beat.
Salsa Caribbean A popular dance music genre that initially arose in New York City during the 1960s from the Cuban son music.
Soukous Congo A dance music genre that originated from Cuban rumba music in the Belgian and French Congo during the 1940s and early 1950s.
Techno Germany Originating from Detroit in the mid-1980s and subsequently adopted by European producers, techno is characterized by repetitive instrumentals and a central rhythmic beat.
Zydeco USA A music genre that evolved in southwest Louisiana by French Creole speakers which blends blues, rhythm and blues, and music indigenous to the Louisiana Creoles and the Native people of Louisiana.
